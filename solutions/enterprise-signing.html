The post-quantum transition

NIST FIPS 204 & 205 Compliant

ML-DSA (Module-Lattice Digital Signature Algorithm) and SLH-DSA (Stateless Hash-based Digital Signature Algorithm). Both finalized NIST standards. Phoenix builds exclusively on these — no proprietary algorithms, no unproven constructions.

Replace RSA & ECDSA

RSA-2048 and ECDSA-256 will be broken by quantum computers. Phoenix provides a direct migration path. DualSign uses both algorithms simultaneously — ML-DSA for performance, SLH-DSA for conservative assurance — until confidence in individual standards is fully established.

Post-Compromise Security

Beyond quantum resistance, Phoenix adds automatic recovery after key compromise. Government and enterprise signing infrastructure that heals itself. No manual key rotation procedures. No emergency response playbooks. The system recovers automatically.

Algorithm Agility

Standards will evolve. NIST will release additional algorithms. Phoenix's GenericPCSWrapper integrates new PQC algorithms within one day — not the 6–12 months competitors require. Your infrastructure adapts without rebuild.

Compliance Timeline

CNSA 2.0 requires post-quantum for National Security Systems by 2030. NIST SP 800-131B deprecates classical algorithms. Federal agencies must transition. Private sector follows. Start now — compliance timelines are shorter than deployment timelines.
